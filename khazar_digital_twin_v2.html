Technical Background
1
Lake-basin water balance framing
Sea-level stress is interpreted from endorheic-basin controls: river inflow, evaporation forcing, precipitation, wind setup and shoreline hypsometry.
2
Remote-sensing evidence separation
Optical, thermal, altimetry, bathymetry and coastline layers remain separate until the export/audit stage, reducing hidden model mixing.
3
Calibration overlay model
Uploaded observations are validated against a fixed metric schema and added as an overlay, preserving the original data layout and baseline interpretation.
4
Scenario delta comparison
Compare mode reports deltas in water level, surface area, inflow health, alert severity and confidence from the same live state.

Marine physics High

Copernicus Marine products supply sea level, SST, salinity and current context. Limitation: gridded products require local validation near shallow northern coastlines.

Climate forcing High

ERA5 supports wind, precipitation and evaporation-pressure interpretation. Limitation: reanalysis cells cannot replace station-level microclimate measurements.

Optical ecology Medium

Sentinel-3 OLCI and NASA Ocean Color inform chlorophyll, turbidity and bloom alerts. Limitation: cloud, aerosol and coastal adjacency flags must be respected.

Hydrology and gauges Medium

GloFAS and GRDC provide river discharge priors for Volga, Ural, Kura and Terek. Limitation: restricted national gauges improve confidence when uploaded.
